# Review Questions

## Primary Question

How do microclimate data sources and coupling strategies shape the uncertainty,
validation, output impacts, and policy-grade credibility of Urban Building
Energy Modeling (UBEM)?

## RQ1. What types of microclimate information are used in UBEM?

Extract the specific microclimate variables or descriptors used by each study.
Examples include:

- air temperature;
- humidity;
- wind speed;
- solar radiation;
- longwave radiation;
- surface temperature;
- land surface temperature;
- tree canopy;
- sky view factor;
- anthropogenic heat;
- urban heat island intensity.

## RQ2. How are microclimate inputs introduced into UBEM?

Extract the data-transformation or model-coupling pathway. Examples include:

- EPW substitution;
- Urban Weather Generator transformation;
- CFD coupling;
- ENVI-met coupling;
- WRF/UCM coupling;
- sensor-based correction;
- remote-sensing correction;
- hybrid observational-simulation workflow.

## RQ3. How much do different microclimate inputs affect UBEM outputs?

Extract reported quantitative or qualitative impacts, and preserve the output
metric and direction of effect. Examples include:

- heating EUI;
- cooling EUI;
- peak load;
- thermal comfort;
- overheating risk;
- retrofit performance;
- carbon emissions.

## RQ4. How are microclimate-integrated UBEM models validated?

Extract the validation data source, scale, metric, and whether validation is
performed for microclimate inputs, energy outputs, indoor conditions, or all of
these. Examples include:

- weather stations;
- mobile sensors;
- IoT sensors;
- smart meters;
- district heating/cooling data;
- measured indoor temperature;
- satellite land surface temperature.

## RQ5. What prevents microclimate-aware UBEM from becoming policy-grade?

Extract barriers that limit transferability, reliability, transparency, and use
in decision making. Examples include:

- spatial mismatch;
- temporal mismatch;
- computational burden;
- uncertainty propagation;
- lack of standardized data schema;
- lack of validation;
- poor transferability across cities and climates.

## Synthesis Logic

The review should synthesize evidence along this chain:

```text
microclimate information type
-> introduction/coupling strategy
-> propagated uncertainty and output impact
-> validation evidence
-> policy-grade readiness or barrier
```

## Unit of Analysis

The primary unit of analysis is a scholarly record, report, preprint, thesis, or
local BibTeX record that can be traced to a raw source entry and explicitly
connects urban microclimate with urban-scale building energy modeling.
