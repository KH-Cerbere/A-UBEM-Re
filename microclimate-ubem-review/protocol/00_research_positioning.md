# Research Positioning

## Working Title

Microclimate-aware Urban Building Energy Modeling: A systematic review of data
sources, coupling strategies, uncertainty propagation, and policy-grade
applications

## Working Review Identity

This review is not a broad review of UBEM and microclimate tools. It focuses on
the causal chain that determines whether microclimate-aware UBEM can support
credible urban energy and climate policy:

```text
microclimate inputs -> modeling coupling -> uncertainty propagation -> output
impacts -> validation evidence -> policy-grade credibility
```

## Boundary

The review is not a general UBEM review and not a standalone urban climate
review. A study is central only when it connects both sides:

- urban, district, stock, neighborhood, or city-scale building energy modeling;
- microclimate, urban climate, urban canopy, heat island, street canyon, local
  climate zone, weather transformation, sensor correction, remote-sensing
  correction, mesoscale climate, or microscale climate representation.

## Intended Contribution

The repository should support a transparent synthesis of:

- what microclimate information is used in UBEM;
- how that information is introduced into building energy models;
- how microclimate inputs affect heating, cooling, comfort, emissions, retrofit,
  and peak-load outputs;
- how microclimate-integrated UBEM is validated with measured environmental,
  indoor, and energy data;
- what prevents current workflows from becoming policy-grade across cities,
  climates, datasets, and decision contexts.

## Journal-Level Positioning

The review should be framed as a methodological and evidence-synthesis paper,
not as a catalog of software. The expected contribution is a structured account
of data sources, coupling strategies, uncertainty propagation, validation, and
policy-grade application readiness. This framing is intended to be closer to the
expectations of journals that value energy-system relevance, building
performance evidence, measurement and validation, and practical advancement of
building-energy science.

## Phase Constraint

Do not draft manuscript claims until the search log, deduplicated records,
screening results, and first evidence matrix are present. Any claim about a
paper must be traceable to the abstract, PDF note, or extraction table.
